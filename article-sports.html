World Cup Qualifiers: Underdog Squad Stuns Defending Champions in 3-2 Thriller

By Carlos Mendez · Sports Reporter, News Online
12 June 202622:45 BST7 min read

CASABLANCA — Morocco produced one of the greatest shocks in recent World Cup qualifying history on Thursday night, defeating defending world champions Argentina 3-2 in front of a delirious capacity crowd of 67,000 at the Stade Mohammed V. A stunning 93rd-minute volley from midfielder Ilyas El Khannous, making only his fourth international start, sealed a victory that reshapes the CAF-CONMEBOL intercontinental qualifying pathway and sends a thunderous message ahead of the 2026 World Cup.

The match began as many had predicted, with Argentina dominating possession and creating the better chances in the opening twenty minutes. Lionel Messi, now 38 and serving as captain in what is widely expected to be his final World Cup qualifying campaign, orchestrated the Argentine attack with his customary elegance. It was Messi who unlocked the Moroccan defence in the 23rd minute, threading a pass through three defenders to Julian Alvarez, who finished clinically into the bottom corner to give the visitors a 1-0 lead. The Argentine contingent in the stadium erupted, and for a spell it appeared that the world champions would cruise to a routine victory.

Morocco, however, had other ideas. Buoyed by a home crowd whose noise levels barely dipped even after the concession, the Atlas Lions responded with ferocious intensity. Their equaliser came in the 38th minute through a moment of individual brilliance from winger Amine Adli, who cut inside from the left flank, beat two defenders with a sharp change of direction, and curled an unstoppable shot past Emiliano Martinez from the edge of the penalty area. The stadium exploded, and the momentum shifted decisively.

"
We showed tonight that we belong at this level. But we have achieved nothing yet. The job is only half done.

— Walid Regragui, Morocco head coach

The second half was a spectacle of end-to-end football that will be replayed in highlight reels for years. Morocco took the lead in the 61st minute when striker Youssef En-Nesyri rose above the Argentine central defenders to head home a pinpoint cross from Achraf Hakimi, sending the home support into a state of near-delirium. Argentina, stung by the prospect of a humiliating defeat, threw everything forward. Their pressure told in the 78th minute when a deflected shot from Enzo Fernandez fell kindly to Lautaro Martinez, who tapped in from close range to level the contest at 2-2. At that stage, with Argentina pushing for a winner and Morocco clinging on, a draw seemed the most likely outcome.

Then came the moment that will be etched into Moroccan football folklore. In the third minute of stoppage time, a partially cleared corner fell to El Khannous on the edge of the area. The 22-year-old Racing Genk midfielder struck the dropping ball with the purest of volleys, catching it on the half-volley and sending it screaming past a motionless Martinez into the top-right corner. The stadium's roof — metaphorically at least — lifted off its hinges. El Khannous was buried under a pile of teammates as the Moroccan bench sprinted onto the pitch in celebration.

"
Morocco were magnificent tonight — full credit to them, to their coach, to their supporters. We will learn and we will respond.

— Lionel Scaloni, Argentina manager

The result throws the CAF-CONMEBOL intercontinental qualifying playoff wide open. Morocco now sit top of their five-team group with seven points from three matches, two points clear of Argentina, who drop to second place. With three matches remaining, including a return fixture in Buenos Aires in September, the Atlas Lions have placed themselves in a commanding position to secure direct qualification.

For the thousands of Moroccan fans who stayed in the stadium long after the final whistle, waving flags and singing the name of their new hero El Khannous, this was more than a football match. It was a declaration that the balance of power in world football is shifting, one stunning volley at a time.
