Accessibility Statement

News Online is committed to making our content accessible to the widest possible audience, including readers with disabilities. This statement describes our approach and the standards we aim to meet.

✓ WCAG 2.1 Level AA — Partial Conformance

1. Our Commitment

We believe that journalism is a public good, and that includes making news accessible to everyone — regardless of ability, device, or connection speed. As a university journalism project, we integrate accessibility into our editorial workflow and web development process from the start, not as an afterthought.

We aim to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 at Level AA. While we are a small student-staffed operation and may not yet meet every criterion, we actively work towards full conformance and welcome feedback on where we fall short.

2. What We Currently Do

2.1 Structure and Semantics

We use semantic HTML5 throughout — proper heading hierarchy (<h1> through <h6>), landmark regions (<nav>, <main>, <footer>), and ARIA labels where appropriate. Screen readers should navigate our pages cleanly.

2.2 Keyboard Navigation

All interactive elements — navigation links, buttons, form controls, share buttons, and the hamburger menu — are fully keyboard-accessible via Tab, Enter, and Escape. We test keyboard navigation on every new feature.

2.3 Colour Contrast

Our colour palette is designed for readability:

  • Body text (#141414 on #ffffff) achieves a contrast ratio of 17.3:1 — well above the WCAG AA requirement of 4.5:1.
  • Secondary text (#545658 on #ffffff) achieves 7.1:1 — above the 4.5:1 minimum.
  • Red accent links (#B80000 on #ffffff) achieve 7.0:1.
  • Footer text on dark background meets or exceeds the 4.5:1 threshold.

We do not rely on colour alone to convey information — links are underlined, required form fields are labelled, and interactive states include non-colour cues (borders, underlines, opacity).

2.4 Responsive Design and Zoom

The site is fully responsive and supports browser zoom up to 200% without loss of content or functionality. Text is set in relative units (rem, em) so it respects user font-size preferences. We do not disable pinch-to-zoom on mobile.

2.5 Images

All images include descriptive alt text. Decorative images (icons in buttons, brand marks) use empty alt="" or are embedded via SVG <symbol> with aria-hidden="true". Photographic images carry meaningful descriptions that convey the image's editorial purpose, not just a label of visible objects.

2.6 Forms

All form controls have associated <label> elements. Required fields are marked. Error states and success messages use both colour and text. The contact form and comment form include clear button labels and keyboard-operable submission.

2.7 Motion

We use a prefers-reduced-motion media query that disables all animations and transitions for users who have enabled this setting in their operating system. All essential functionality remains available without motion.

2.8 Focus Indicators

Interactive elements have visible focus indicators. We use the browser's default focus ring supplemented by custom hover and focus-visible styles where needed.

3. Known Limitations

We are aware of the following areas that do not yet fully meet WCAG 2.1 AA. We are working to address them:

  • Video captions: Embedded video content in the "Watch & Listen" section does not currently have synchronised captions or audio descriptions. We are exploring automated captioning solutions.
  • Complex data visualisations: The "Have Your Say" animated bar chart and trend chart rely on visual presentation; a text-based data table alternative is available but could be more clearly signposted.
  • Third-party embeds: Social media share buttons and any external embedded content may not fully meet our accessibility standards; this is outside our direct control.
  • Skip link: We do not yet provide a "Skip to main content" link at the top of each page. This is on our roadmap.

4. Testing

We test accessibility through a combination of methods:

  • Automated audits using browser DevTools (Lighthouse) and axe-core.
  • Manual keyboard-only navigation testing on every new page and feature.
  • Screen reader testing with NVDA (Windows) and VoiceOver (macOS/iOS).
  • Colour contrast verification using the WebAIM Contrast Checker.

Our most recent accessibility audit was conducted on 1 June 2026.

5. Feedback and Contact

6. Enforcement

If you are not satisfied with our response to an accessibility concern, you may contact the university's Disability Support Services office or the relevant equality body in your jurisdiction. In Malaysia, accessibility-related complaints may be directed to the Persons with Disabilities Department (Jabatan Kebajikan Masyarakat).
