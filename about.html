About Us

News Online is an independent digital newsroom produced by the School of Journalism and Communication at Xiamen University Malaysia. We report, write, and edit every story with the conviction that fact-based journalism strengthens the communities it serves.

Founded in 2020 as part of the JRN209 Advanced Reporting module, News Online has grown from a classroom exercise into a fully-fledged digital publication covering politics, business, technology, health, climate, sports, culture, and the arts. Our reporters are final-year journalism students working under faculty editorial supervision; every byline you see represents months of fieldwork, source development, and rigorous fact-checking.

Our Mission

We believe public-interest journalism is a civic good, not a commodity. Every article we publish is free to read, free of paywalls, and funded by the university as an educational and public-service commitment. We do not carry advertising, sponsored content, or native marketing — our only obligation is to the reader and the facts.

Our editorial independence is guaranteed by the XMUM Journalism Charter, which ensures that no university administrator, donor, or external party can review or suppress content before publication. Faculty editors hold final editorial authority; student reporters own their bylines.

Editorial Standards

Every story published on News Online must clear a five-step verification desk before it goes live:

1. Source check — At least two named, on-record sources per factual claim; anonymous sourcing requires faculty editor sign-off.
2. Document review — Primary documents (legislation text, scientific papers, financial filings, court records) are read in full, not summarised from press releases.
3. Data audit — Any statistic or percentage cited is traced to its original dataset; we publish the source URL in the article footer.
4. Legal read — Stories involving active litigation, national security, or personal privacy are reviewed by the university's legal advisory panel.
5. Style copy-edit — Final pass for grammar, house style, and accessibility compliance before CMS publication.

Corrections are published prominently at the top of any article found to contain factual error, with a dated note explaining what was changed and why. Readers can submit correction requests to corrections@newsonline.jrn209.xmum.
