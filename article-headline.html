Kuala Lumpur International Mobility Show 2026 Presents New Auto Market Trend

By Xiao Ziyue · Tech & AI Reporter, News Online
21 June 202614:30 MYT5 min read

KUALA LUMPUR: The Kuala Lumpur International Mobility Show (KLIMS) 2026 showed up this week from June 12 to 21 at MITEC, featuring models with brand-new market trend.

After two years of strategic interval, almost 20 domestic and international automotive manufacturers and brands from Malaysia, Japan and China attended the KLIMS this year, exhibiting dozens of unique vehicles, of which many are making their Southeast Asian debut.

Together with car makers, KLIMS 2026 is embracing with new trend of sustainable, intelligent and interconnected trip ecosystem, making the structural shift from simplex “motor” to universal “mobility”.

As the principal sponsor brand, Malaysia’s top national automaker Perodua is generating substantial public interest on this new trend with the exclusive unveiling of its “Chapter 2” (C2) concept electric car.

Model C2, released on June 11 at an advancing media conference and exhibited during following event days, is indicating that Perodua’s forward-looking visions of a Malaysian mobility future is both accessible and highly advanced and tries to open a “new chapter” of Malaysia EVs.

“
The C2 is part of our ongoing EV storyline. It expands our narrative of made by Malaysians for the world as we improve the future of mobility experience for our customers.

— Dato’ Sri Zainal Abidin Ahmad, Perodua President & CEO

Being a B-segment prototype, C2 is equipped with many advanced AI driving assistant mechanisms, including real-time charging station navigation, enhanced anti-theft features, and assisted auto parking.

What’s more, an integrated P-Circle super app is set in C2’s vehicle console, enabling owners to remotely intelligently control and adjust vehicle status and parameters, while integrating the vehicle into an integrated connected device ecosystem.

“We have greatly integrated our P-Circle super app into this prototype so that our customers can access a world of convenience — from a secure e-wallet app to priority genuine parts delivery — the C2 represents the future in mobility integration,” Zainal added.

At the same time, Perodua also promoted its full product line, showcasing its latest QV-E models with discount as well as the new smart wearable devices in an effort to attract new customers.

“
The presence of advanced technology shows the rapid progress of AI adoption in the Southeast Asian automotive industry — the regional market is intentionally moving away from pure mechanical engineering.

— Market observer, KLIMS 2026

International brands are also following to response to rapid market change, such as Chinese EV pioneer XPENG firstly bringing out its latest “Iron” humanoid robots, bionic charging arms and XPeng X2 conceptual flying car in Southeast Asian market.

The high-tech displays underscore the brand’s push to be at the leading edge of next-generation, software-driven transport infrastructure in the region.

TQ Wuling is another Chinese car brand which just entered Malaysian market in 2025, and they appeared in KLIMS 2026 with distinguished products as electric SUVs, vans and mobility scooters.

“We’re just entering SEA market less than one year, and the brand aims at bringing high-cost performance of Chinese Wuling to Malaysian customers with eye-catching products,” a TQ Wuling salesman explained.

Long before becoming a global stage for these modern electric contenders, KLIMS begun in the late 1970s and early 1980s, which has historically been the main commercial platform for Malaysia’s automotive sector.

With more than 40 years of history, it has already held 11 editions of events until today and released countless classical vehicle models, such as Proton Saga Megavalve 1990 and Perodua Buddyz 2013, witnessing the development of national and international automotives.

The 11th edition, which comes after the 2024 edition, is a deliberate and decisive shift away from the traditional fossil fuel era.

Industry leaders say the cutting-edge software and mobility solutions on display at the venue will soon set regional manufacturing standards.

Hence, the path-breaking innovations displayed on the exhibition floor this week are bound to create a whole new benchmark for road networks in South-East Asia.
